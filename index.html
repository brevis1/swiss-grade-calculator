Understanding the Swiss Grading System

In Switzerland, schools typically use a scale from 1.0 (lowest) to 6.0 (highest). A score of 4.0 or above is usually required to pass. Some cantons or universities may adopt variations, but the 1–6 scale is the national norm. This numerical simplicity helps maintain transparency and comparability across institutions.

Grades represent mastery of course objectives: 6.0 indicates exceptional performance, 5.0 solid mastery, 4.0 satisfactory understanding, and below 4.0 insufficient results. Teachers often round grades to one decimal place, for example, 5.4 or 4.8.

Simple Average Method

If each test or assignment has equal importance, compute the mean value:

Formula: (Grade₁ + Grade₂ + ... + Gradeₙ) / n

Example: (5.0 + 4.5 + 5.5) / 3 = 5.0. This works best when no single exam is more significant than others.

Weighted Average Method

When assessments have different weights (for instance, finals count double), apply the weighted average formula:

Formula: (g₁×w₁ + g₂×w₂ + ... + gₙ×wₙ) / (w₁ + w₂ + ... + wₙ)

Example: Exam A (5.5×2), Exam B (4.0×1), Project (5.0×3) → (11 + 4 + 15) / 6 = 5.0.

Conversion Between Systems

Swiss to ECTS

Some universities convert numeric Swiss grades into ECTS letters as follows:

  • 6.0–5.5 → A
  • 5.4–4.6 → B
  • 4.5–4.0 → C
  • 3.9–3.0 → D/E (depending on policy)

Swiss to GPA (U.S. style)

Approximate conversion for reference only:

  • 6.0 → 4.0 GPA
  • 5.0 → 3.0 GPA
  • 4.0 → 2.0 GPA

Because no single method fits all, always confirm conversion rules with the receiving institution.

Practical Tips

  • Confirm weights and rounding rules early — they can affect results significantly.
  • Use two decimal precision during computation and round only the final average.
  • Track retake policies; sometimes the new grade replaces the old, sometimes they average.
  • Store calculations in spreadsheets for transparency.

Official Guidance

Each canton’s education department provides detailed assessment frameworks. For reference on education coordination in Switzerland, see the Swiss Conference of Cantonal Ministers of Education, which oversees curriculum harmonization and grading principles across cantons.

Step-by-Step Checklist

  1. Verify your institution’s grading scale (1–6) and passing threshold (usually 4.0).
  2. Collect all component grades and their weights.
  3. Choose between simple or weighted average calculation.
  4. Perform the computation and cross-check with an online calculator.
  5. Keep documentation for grade verification or appeal if needed.
